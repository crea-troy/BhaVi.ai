# ── MATHEMATICS ───────────────────────────────────────────────────

Mathematics is the language of the universe. It describes patterns, structures, and relationships that exist independently of human thought.

Arithmetic is the foundation of mathematics. Addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division are the four basic operations. Every number system builds on these fundamentals.

Algebra uses symbols and variables to represent unknown quantities. An equation like x plus 5 equals 12 means x equals 7. Algebra lets us solve problems without knowing all values in advance.

Calculus was invented independently by Newton and Leibniz in the 17th century. It has two branches: differential calculus studies rates of change, and integral calculus studies accumulation. The derivative of position is velocity. The derivative of velocity is acceleration. Integration is the reverse of differentiation.

Geometry studies shapes, sizes, and properties of figures in space. Euclid's Elements established the foundations of geometry around 300 BCE. The Pythagorean theorem states that in a right triangle, a squared plus b squared equals c squared, where c is the hypotenuse.

Statistics and probability describe uncertainty. The mean is the average. The standard deviation measures spread. Probability ranges from 0 (impossible) to 1 (certain). Bayes theorem updates probability based on new evidence.

Prime numbers are integers greater than 1 with no divisors other than 1 and themselves. There are infinitely many prime numbers. The first few primes are 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17, 19, 23.

Pi is the ratio of a circle's circumference to its diameter. Pi equals approximately 3.14159265. Pi is irrational — its decimal expansion never repeats or terminates.

Euler's identity states e to the power of i times pi plus 1 equals 0. It connects five fundamental mathematical constants: e, i, pi, 1, and 0. Many mathematicians consider it the most beautiful equation in mathematics.

# ── PHYSICS ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Physics is the study of matter, energy, space, and time. It seeks the fundamental laws governing the universe.

Classical mechanics describes the motion of objects. Newton's three laws govern everyday motion. Objects move in straight lines unless forces act on them. Force equals mass times acceleration. Every action has an equal and opposite reaction.

Energy exists in many forms: kinetic, potential, thermal, chemical, nuclear, electromagnetic. Energy is always conserved — it transforms but never disappears.

Thermodynamics studies heat and energy transfer. Temperature measures average kinetic energy of particles. Entropy measures disorder. The universe tends toward increasing entropy.

Electromagnetism describes electricity and magnetism as a unified force. Electric charges create electric fields. Moving charges create magnetic fields. Light is an electromagnetic wave. The speed of light is approximately 299,792,458 meters per second.

Special relativity changed our understanding of space and time. Time passes more slowly for moving objects. Length contracts in the direction of motion. Mass and energy are equivalent: E equals mc squared. Nothing with mass can reach the speed of light.

General relativity describes gravity as the curvature of spacetime caused by mass and energy. Massive objects warp the fabric of spacetime. Light bends around massive objects. Black holes are regions where spacetime curvature becomes infinite.

Quantum mechanics describes the behavior of matter at atomic and subatomic scales. Particles have wave-like properties. The act of measurement affects what is measured. Particles exist in superposition of states until observed. Quantum entanglement allows particles to be correlated regardless of distance.

The Standard Model describes three of the four fundamental forces: electromagnetic, weak nuclear, and strong nuclear. It classifies elementary particles into quarks, leptons, and bosons. The Higgs boson gives particles their mass.

Dark matter and dark energy make up approximately 95 percent of the universe. Dark matter does not emit light but exerts gravitational effects. Dark energy drives the accelerating expansion of the universe.

# ── CHEMISTRY ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Chemistry studies matter, its properties, composition, and transformations.

The atom is the basic unit of matter. Atoms consist of a nucleus containing protons and neutrons, surrounded by electrons. The number of protons defines the element.

The periodic table organizes elements by atomic number and chemical properties. There are 118 known elements. Hydrogen is the lightest and most abundant element in the universe. Carbon is the basis of all known life.

Chemical bonds form when atoms share or transfer electrons. Covalent bonds involve shared electrons. Ionic bonds involve electron transfer. Hydrogen bonds are weak attractions important in water and biological molecules.

Water is essential for life. Its chemical formula is H2O. Water has unusual properties due to hydrogen bonding: high surface tension, high heat capacity, and the fact that ice is less dense than liquid water.

Acids have pH below 7 and donate protons. Bases have pH above 7 and accept protons. Neutral solutions have pH of 7. The pH scale is logarithmic — each unit represents a tenfold change.

# ── BIOLOGY ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Biology is the study of living organisms and their interactions with the environment.

The cell is the basic unit of life. Prokaryotic cells lack a nucleus. Eukaryotic cells have a membrane-bound nucleus. All known life is made of cells.

DNA is the molecule that carries genetic information. It has a double helix structure discovered by Watson and Crick in 1953. DNA contains four bases: adenine, thymine, guanine, and cytosine. Genes are sequences of DNA that encode proteins.

Evolution by natural selection is the central organizing principle of biology. Charles Darwin proposed it in 1859. Organisms with traits better suited to their environment reproduce more. Over generations, populations change. All life on Earth shares a common ancestor.

The human body contains approximately 37 trillion cells. The brain has roughly 86 billion neurons. The human genome contains about 3 billion base pairs encoding approximately 20,000 genes.

Photosynthesis converts light energy into chemical energy. Plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to produce glucose and oxygen. This process sustains almost all life on Earth.

# ── COMPUTER SCIENCE & AI ─────────────────────────────────────────

Computer science studies computation, algorithms, and information processing.

An algorithm is a step-by-step procedure for solving a problem. Good algorithms are correct, efficient, and clear. Sorting, searching, and graph traversal are fundamental algorithm categories.

Programming languages allow humans to instruct computers. Python is known for readability and versatility. JavaScript runs in web browsers. C and C++ offer low-level control and high performance. Each language has strengths for different tasks.

Machine learning enables computers to learn from data without explicit programming. Neural networks are inspired by biological brains. Deep learning uses many layers of neural networks. Large language models like GPT learn to predict and generate text.

Artificial intelligence aims to create systems that can perform tasks requiring human-like intelligence. Current AI excels at pattern recognition, language processing, and game playing. True general intelligence remains an open research challenge.

The internet is a global network of connected computers. HTTP is the protocol for web communication. HTML structures web pages. Encryption protects data in transit. The web has transformed communication, commerce, and knowledge sharing.

# ── ASTRONOMY ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Astronomy studies celestial objects, space, and the universe as a whole.

The universe is approximately 13.8 billion years old. It began with the Big Bang — a rapid expansion from an extremely hot, dense state. The universe continues to expand and accelerate.

Our solar system formed about 4.6 billion years ago. The Sun is a medium-sized star containing 99.86 percent of the solar system's mass. Eight planets orbit the Sun: Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.

Stars are massive balls of plasma that generate energy through nuclear fusion. The Sun fuses hydrogen into helium. Massive stars end their lives in supernova explosions, creating elements heavier than iron.

A light-year is the distance light travels in one year — approximately 9.46 trillion kilometers. The nearest star to Earth is Proxima Centauri at 4.24 light-years away. The Milky Way galaxy is about 100,000 light-years across.

Black holes form when massive stars collapse. Their gravity is so strong that not even light can escape. The event horizon is the boundary beyond which nothing returns. Supermassive black holes exist at the centers of most galaxies.

# ── HISTORY ───────────────────────────────────────────────────────

History is the study of past human events and civilizations.

Ancient civilizations arose independently in Mesopotamia, Egypt, the Indus Valley, and China. They developed writing, agriculture, cities, and complex social structures.

Ancient Greece gave us democracy, philosophy, mathematics, and science. Socrates, Plato, and Aristotle laid foundations of Western philosophy. Euclid, Pythagoras, and Archimedes advanced mathematics and physics.

The Roman Empire united much of Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. Rome spread Latin language, law, engineering, and culture. The fall of Rome in 476 CE marks the beginning of the Middle Ages.

The Renaissance was a cultural rebirth in Europe from the 14th to 17th centuries. It celebrated human achievement in art, science, and literature. Leonardo da Vinci, Michelangelo, Galileo, and Copernicus transformed human understanding.

The Scientific Revolution in the 16th and 17th centuries transformed our understanding of nature. Copernicus placed the Sun at the center of the solar system. Galileo used experiments and mathematics. Newton unified terrestrial and celestial mechanics.

The Industrial Revolution began in Britain in the late 18th century. Steam power, mechanized production, and railways transformed economies. It led to urbanization, rising living standards, and environmental challenges.

The 20th century saw two World Wars, the development of nuclear weapons, the space race, and the rise of digital technology. The internet connected the world. Humanity landed on the Moon in 1969.

# ── PHILOSOPHY ────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Philosophy asks fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, ethics, and reason.

Epistemology studies knowledge. What can we know? How do we know it? Rationalists believe reason is the primary source of knowledge. Empiricists believe knowledge comes from experience and observation.

Metaphysics studies the nature of reality. What exists? What is consciousness? What is the relationship between mind and matter? These questions remain open after millennia of inquiry.

Ethics studies right and wrong. Consequentialism judges actions by their outcomes. Deontology judges actions by their adherence to rules or duties. Virtue ethics focuses on character and human flourishing.

Logic is the study of valid reasoning. An argument is valid if its conclusion follows necessarily from its premises. A sound argument is valid and has true premises. Formal logic uses symbols to represent and evaluate arguments.

The philosophy of science asks how science works and what scientific knowledge means. Karl Popper argued that scientific theories must be falsifiable. Thomas Kuhn described science as proceeding through paradigm shifts.

# ── GEOGRAPHY ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Geography studies Earth's physical features, climate, and human societies.

Earth has seven continents: Africa, Antarctica, Asia, Australia, Europe, North America, and South America. Asia is the largest by area and population. Australia is the smallest continent.

Earth has five oceans: the Pacific, Atlantic, Indian, Southern, and Arctic. The Pacific is the largest, covering more area than all land combined.

The atmosphere is divided into layers: troposphere, stratosphere, mesosphere, thermosphere, and exosphere. Weather occurs in the troposphere. The ozone layer in the stratosphere protects life from ultraviolet radiation.

Climate change is the long-term shift in global temperatures and weather patterns. Human activities, particularly burning fossil fuels, have increased greenhouse gas concentrations. Global average temperature has risen approximately 1.1 degrees Celsius since the industrial era.

# ── LANGUAGES & CULTURE ───────────────────────────────────────────

There are approximately 7,000 languages spoken in the world today. Mandarin Chinese has the most native speakers. English is the most widely spoken second language and the dominant language of science, technology, and international communication.

Language shapes thought and culture. Different languages have different structures, sounds, and ways of expressing ideas. Learning multiple languages improves cognitive flexibility and cultural understanding.

Art, music, and literature are expressions of human creativity and culture. They communicate emotions, ideas, and experiences that transcend language. Every culture produces unique artistic traditions.

# ── HEALTH & MEDICINE ─────────────────────────────────────────────

Medicine is the science and practice of diagnosing, treating, and preventing disease.

The human immune system defends against pathogens. Vaccines train the immune system to recognize and fight specific diseases. Antibiotics treat bacterial infections but are ineffective against viruses.

The brain controls all body functions. It has regions specialized for movement, sensation, language, memory, and emotion. Sleep is essential for memory consolidation and brain health.

Good health requires balanced nutrition, regular physical activity, adequate sleep, and stress management. A diet rich in vegetables, fruits, whole grains, and lean proteins supports health. Regular exercise reduces risk of heart disease, diabetes, and depression.

Mental health is as important as physical health. Anxiety and depression are among the most common health conditions worldwide. Therapy, medication, and lifestyle changes can effectively treat mental health conditions.

# ── ECONOMICS ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Economics studies how people allocate scarce resources.

Supply and demand determine prices in markets. When supply exceeds demand, prices fall. When demand exceeds supply, prices rise. Markets coordinate the decisions of millions of individuals.

Gross Domestic Product measures the total value of goods and services produced in a country. Economic growth, inflation, unemployment, and interest rates are key macroeconomic indicators.

Capitalism is an economic system based on private ownership and market competition. Socialism emphasizes collective ownership and state planning. Most modern economies are mixed systems combining elements of both.
